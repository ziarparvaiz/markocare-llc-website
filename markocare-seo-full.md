### Content Brief

Cancer changes everything — for the person diagnosed and for every family member surrounding them. In-home cancer care support fills a critical gap: the medical team treats the disease, but someone still needs to be there at home, helping with the daily realities of life during treatment.

MarkoCare provides non-medical in-home support specifically designed around the needs and rhythms of cancer patients in Maryland. Our RSA-licensed caregivers work alongside oncology teams, hospital discharge planners, and family members to ensure the person receiving care can focus on healing — not logistics.

**What We Do:** During active treatment (chemotherapy, radiation, immunotherapy), cancer patients often experience profound fatigue, nausea, mobility challenges, and cognitive fog. Our caregivers assist with bathing, dressing, meal preparation, light housekeeping, and medication reminders. We provide transportation to and from treatment appointments so families don't have to rearrange their entire work schedules.

**Emotional Support:** Beyond physical tasks, our caregivers offer consistent, warm companionship. Cancer is isolating. Having a trained, calm presence in the home several hours per day can meaningfully reduce anxiety and depression in both patients and their family members.

**Post-Surgery Recovery:** For patients recovering from surgical procedures — lumpectomy, mastectomy, colostomy, prostatectomy, and others — our caregivers assist with wound care observation (alerting clinicians to changes), mobility support, and maintaining the home environment during recovery.

**For Family Caregivers:** We also offer structured respite so family caregivers can work, rest, and tend to their own health without guilt or worry. Our coordination team is available 24/7 for schedule changes or urgent needs.

We serve cancer patients across Howard, Anne Arundel, Carroll, and Frederick Counties in Maryland. Call today for a compassionate, no-pressure consultation. Target: 700–800 words.

### Content Brief

Social isolation is one of the greatest unaddressed health risks facing Maryland seniors. Studies consistently show that loneliness accelerates cognitive decline, worsens depression, and increases mortality risk in older adults. Companion care is not a luxury — it is a meaningful health intervention delivered through human connection.

MarkoCare's companion caregivers are warm, patient, engaging individuals who show up consistently to be a trusted presence in a senior's life. They are not just helpers; they are companions in the truest sense.

**What Companion Caregivers Do:** Our Maryland companion caregivers engage seniors in conversation, hobbies, games, reading, and light outdoor walks. They help with light meal preparation — nothing clinical, just making sure someone has a warm lunch and a friendly face across the table. They run errands, accompany seniors to doctor's appointments, hair salons, religious services, or family events.

**Safe Supervision:** Even without hands-on personal care, companion caregivers maintain awareness of fall risks, behavioral changes, and medication schedules. They are the eyes and ears in the home, reporting concerns to family members and the MarkoCare coordination team promptly.

**Who This Is For:** Companion care is ideal for seniors living alone in Howard County, Anne Arundel County, Carroll County, or Frederick County who are cognitively intact but benefit from regular social interaction. It is also a powerful first step for families who are not yet ready to introduce hands-on personal care, and a gentle transition for individuals with early-stage memory changes who still want to maintain independence.

**Caregiver Matching:** MarkoCare takes caregiver matching seriously. We consider personality, interests, background, and communication style to pair seniors with someone they will genuinely look forward to seeing. Because consistency matters. Target: 650–750 words.

**Q1: What is home care, and how is it different from a nursing home?**
Home care allows your loved one to receive personalized assistance in the comfort of their own home — their familiar surroundings, their routines, their independence. A nursing home is a facility-based setting for those who need 24-hour institutional care. Most seniors strongly prefer to remain at home, and with the right support, many can do so safely for years longer than families realize.

**Q2: What is an RSA, and why does it matter?**
RSA stands for Residential Service Agency — a designation regulated by the Maryland Department of Health under COMAR 10.07.05. RSA agencies like MarkoCare must meet state standards for staff training, caregiver supervision, care plan development, and quality assurance. This licensure protects you. Registries and unlicensed referral services are not held to the same standards.

**Q3: What is the difference between a home care agency and a home care registry?**
An agency like MarkoCare employs caregivers directly — we handle hiring, training, supervision, insurance, taxes, and liability. A registry simply matches you with an independent contractor whom you are then responsible for managing, paying, and potentially insuring. If an uninsured registry caregiver is injured in your home, you may bear liability. With MarkoCare, that risk is ours.

**Q4: Are MarkoCare caregivers background-checked?**
Yes. Every MarkoCare caregiver undergoes criminal background screening, reference verification, skills assessment, and orientation training before being assigned to any client. Ongoing performance reviews and RN supervision are standard practice.

**Q5: How does MarkoCare match me with a caregiver?**
Matching is not random. We consider care needs, personality, shared interests, language, schedule, and location. Our goal is a relationship — not just a shift. If a match isn't working, we address it promptly.

**Q6: How much does home care cost in Maryland?**
Home care rates vary by service type, hours, and frequency. MarkoCare offers transparent private-pay pricing — no hidden fees, no surprise charges. We provide a written cost summary before care begins. We are currently private pay; Medicaid-ready infrastructure is in development.

**Q7: Does MarkoCare accept Medicare or Medicaid?**
Currently, MarkoCare operates on a private-pay model. We are developing Medicaid-ready infrastructure. Medicare does not cover non-medical home care. If cost is a concern, our care coordinators can help you explore all available options.

**Q8: How quickly can care begin?**
Often within 24–48 hours of an initial consultation. For post-hospital discharge situations, we can coordinate rapid-start care. Contact us as soon as you know a care need is coming — earlier is always better.

**Q9: What if I only need a few hours a week?**
MarkoCare accommodates a range of schedules — from a few hours of companion care per week to full-time daily care. Our care coordinator will discuss minimum scheduling requirements and find a plan that fits your needs and budget.

**Q10: Can MarkoCare work with my loved one's doctor or hospital team?**
Yes. MarkoCare's RN supervisor coordinates with physicians, discharge planners, and case managers. We are experienced in Maryland's healthcare referral ecosystem and welcome collaborative care relationships.

**Q11: What happens if my caregiver is sick or unavailable?**
MarkoCare maintains a trained backup staffing pool. If your assigned caregiver cannot make a scheduled shift, we notify you in advance and arrange qualified coverage. Your care is never left without support.

**Q12: What counties does MarkoCare serve?**
We currently serve Howard, Anne Arundel, Carroll, and Frederick Counties in Maryland. If you are unsure whether your community is within our service area, call us — we will confirm coverage.

### Content Brief

This page speaks directly to a professional audience — hospital discharge planners, social workers, case managers, physicians, and other healthcare providers who make home care referrals on behalf of their patients. Clinicians and care coordinators do not need emotional storytelling — they need to know that a referral to MarkoCare will be handled competently and promptly, and that their patient will be safe.

**The Core Value Proposition for Referral Partners:**
1. **Reliability:** MarkoCare responds to referrals promptly. We contact referred families within hours, not days. When you send a patient to MarkoCare, we follow up — always.
2. **Licensure and Supervision:** As a Maryland RSA (COMAR 10.07.05) with RN-level supervision, MarkoCare meets the regulatory and clinical standards that discharge planners need to feel confident in a home care referral.
3. **Communication:** We maintain documentation practices and communication standards that keep referring providers informed. Care plans are available upon request. Significant changes in a client's status are communicated to the referring provider when clinically appropriate.
4. **Coverage:** Howard, Anne Arundel, Carroll, and Frederick Counties — covering the primary referral footprint of Maryland's central and western hospital systems.

**Referral Process:** Make it as frictionless as possible. Describe a simple referral submission — phone, email, or online form. Set the expectation: MarkoCare will contact the family or patient representative within hours, complete an assessment, develop a care plan, and start care — with a status report back to the referring party.

**Conditions Commonly Referred:** Post-surgical recovery, stroke, cardiac events, COPD exacerbation, dementia with behavioral challenges, fall recovery, and cancer treatment support.

Tone: Professional, peer-level, confidence-building. No sentimentality. Target: 550–700 words.
