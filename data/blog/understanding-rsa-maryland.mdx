If you are researching home care options in Maryland, you may have encountered the term "Residential Service Agency" or its abbreviation, RSA. Understanding what this designation means — and why it matters — can help you make a more informed decision when choosing a care provider for your family.

## What Is a Residential Service Agency?

A Residential Service Agency (RSA) is a home care organization licensed by the Maryland Department of Health to provide nonmedical personal care services to individuals in their homes. RSA licensure is the regulatory framework under which legitimate home care companies in Maryland operate.

To become licensed as an RSA, an agency must meet detailed requirements established by the Maryland Department of Health, including:

- Background checks on all employees (state and federal criminal history, sex offender registry, OIG exclusion list)
- Minimum training standards for aides
- Registered Nurse supervision of all client care
- Documented care plans for each client
- Compliance with Maryland's employment and consumer protection laws

## Why RSA Licensure Matters

Without RSA licensure, a home care company is not authorized to provide personal care services in Maryland. However, the reality is that unlicensed providers operate throughout the state — often advertising on caregiver matching platforms, community boards, and social media.

Unlicensed providers pose real risks to families:

**No required background checks.** An RSA must screen every employee before placement. Unlicensed providers have no such obligation — meaning a caregiver with a criminal history could enter your parent's home without your knowledge.

**No RN supervision.** RSA regulations require that a registered nurse assess each client and supervise all care. Unlicensed providers have no clinical oversight. Changes in your loved one's health may go unrecognized and unreported.

**No accountability.** If something goes wrong with an unlicensed provider, your recourse is limited. Licensed agencies are subject to state oversight, consumer complaint processes, and regulatory enforcement.

**Liability and tax risk.** When a family hires a caregiver directly — even through a platform — they may legally be considered an employer. This creates potential exposure for payroll taxes, unemployment insurance, and workers' compensation.

## The W2 Difference

Within the RSA category, there is another important distinction: whether a company employs its caregivers as W2 employees or classifies them as independent contractors.

W2 employment means the agency:

- Withholds taxes on the caregiver's behalf
- Carries workers' compensation and liability insurance
- Maintains direct supervisory control over the caregiver
- Can discipline or replace a caregiver who is not performing well

At MarkoCare, all caregivers are W2 employees supervised by a registered nurse. This structure offers families the highest level of accountability, clinical oversight, and protection.

## What to Ask When Evaluating a Home Care Provider

Before engaging any home care company in Maryland, we recommend asking:

1. **Are you licensed as a Residential Service Agency by the Maryland Department of Health?**
2. **Are your caregivers W2 employees or independent contractors?**
3. **Are they supervised by a registered nurse?**
4. **What does your background screening process include?**
5. **What training do your aides complete before working with clients?**

A reputable agency will answer these questions clearly and directly. If a provider is vague, evasive, or unable to provide a license number, that is a meaningful red flag.
