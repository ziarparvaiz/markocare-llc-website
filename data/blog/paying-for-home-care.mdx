Cost is one of the first concerns families raise when considering professional home care — and understandably so. Home care is a meaningful investment, and the financial landscape can be confusing.

The good news is that most families have more options than they realize. This guide walks through the primary funding sources available to Maryland families.

## Private Pay

The most straightforward option is paying for home care directly out of savings, retirement accounts, or family contributions. Many families use a combination of a senior's Social Security income, pension, and savings to cover care costs.

Private-pay care offers maximum flexibility — you choose the agency, the schedule, and the services without navigating eligibility requirements or benefit approval processes.

## Long-Term Care Insurance

If your loved one purchased a long-term care (LTC) insurance policy earlier in life, it may now be available to pay for home care. LTC policies vary widely in:

- What triggers the benefit (typically inability to perform two or more activities of daily living)
- The daily or monthly benefit amount
- Whether there is an inflation adjustment
- The elimination period (a waiting period before benefits begin)

Review the policy documents carefully or contact the insurance company to understand what is covered and how to file a claim. Many families are unaware that their parent holds a policy, so it is worth searching through financial documents.

## Veterans Benefits

Veterans and their surviving spouses may be eligible for significant home care assistance through the Department of Veterans Affairs:

**Aid and Attendance Pension:** A VA pension supplement specifically designed for veterans who need help with daily activities such as bathing, dressing, and eating. The monthly benefit amount varies based on family status and can be substantial.

**Veteran-Directed Care Program:** Available in some areas, this program allows eligible veterans to manage their own VA-funded home care services.

Eligibility for VA benefits is based on military service history and financial need. The application process can be complex. A VA-accredited claims agent or the Howard County Veterans Services Office can help you navigate it.

## Maryland Medicaid and Waiver Programs

Medicaid is an income- and asset-based program, but several Maryland Medicaid waiver programs are specifically designed to support home-based care for older adults who might otherwise require nursing facility placement:

**Community First Choice (CFC):** Provides personal assistance services (help with bathing, dressing, mobility) and other supports in the home for Medicaid-eligible individuals. There is no waiting list for this program.

**Community Personal Assistance Services (CPAS):** Covers personal care and companion services for qualifying older adults and adults with disabilities.

To qualify for most Medicaid waiver programs, an individual must meet both financial eligibility criteria (income and asset limits) and functional eligibility criteria (typically needing help with multiple activities of daily living).

The Maryland Department of Health and Howard County's Office on Aging and Independence can assist families in assessing eligibility and submitting applications.

## Spend-Down and Medicaid Planning

Many families believe their assets are too high for Medicaid eligibility. In some cases this is true; in others, proper Medicaid planning — often with the guidance of an elder law attorney — can preserve a portion of a family's assets while establishing eligibility for care funding.

This is a complex area with significant legal and financial implications. We recommend consulting a Maryland-licensed elder law attorney before making large financial decisions for the purpose of Medicaid eligibility.

## Life Insurance Conversion Options

Some life insurance policies can be converted into funds for long-term care. Options include:

- **Life settlement:** Selling a life insurance policy to a third party for a lump sum greater than the cash surrender value
- **Accelerated death benefits:** Some policies allow the policyholder to access a portion of the death benefit early if they have a chronic illness

These options depend heavily on the specific policy and should be evaluated with a financial advisor.

## What Home Care Actually Costs

Home care is priced by the hour in Maryland, with rates varying based on the type of care, the agency, and the hours needed. Companion care (non-personal) is typically priced lower than personal care (assistance with bathing, dressing, toileting).

When comparing costs, consider not just the hourly rate but what is included: RN supervision, a formal care plan, regular quality reviews, and 24/7 on-call support are elements that distinguish a professional licensed agency from an informal arrangement.
