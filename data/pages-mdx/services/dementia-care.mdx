Dementia does not follow a predictable path. Each person's experience with Alzheimer's or another form of cognitive decline is shaped by their personality, their history, their relationships, and the specific nature of their disease. Effective dementia care is never one-size-fits-all — it requires caregivers who understand the disease, know the individual, and respond with patience and skill to whatever each day brings.

## A Dementia-Informed Approach

MarkoCare caregivers who work with dementia clients receive specialized training that goes beyond standard care protocols. This training covers the stages of dementia progression, validated communication techniques such as validation therapy, non-confrontational redirection strategies, and safe management of common behavioral symptoms including agitation, sundowning, and wandering.

Our RN works closely with each dementia client's family and physician to ensure the care plan reflects the current stage of the disease and anticipates what is coming next.

## Creating a Safe and Calming Home Environment

One of the most important factors in dementia care is the home environment itself. Our RN conducts a thorough home safety assessment at the start of care — identifying and addressing hazards such as unsecured exits, fall risks, kitchen dangers, and medication storage. Small environmental modifications can dramatically reduce stress and risk for dementia clients.

We also work with families on how to structure routines and set up familiar spaces in ways that reduce confusion and promote a sense of calm and security.

## Supporting the Family Caregiver

Dementia affects the entire family — not just the person diagnosed. The emotional toll on family caregivers is real and significant. MarkoCare's dementia care services give family members relief, support, and an informed partner in navigating one of life's most demanding caregiving journeys.

We keep families informed about changes in behavior or health status and are always available to discuss care decisions, concerns, and next steps.

## When Care Needs Increase

Dementia is progressive. What is adequate care today may need to be adjusted in three months. Our RN conducts regular supervisory visits to monitor the client's cognitive status and update the care plan accordingly. If the time comes when in-home care is no longer sufficient, we will be honest with your family and help you understand what options are available.

Dementia care is available across Howard, Carroll, Anne Arundel, and Frederick Counties, Maryland.
