Social connection is not a luxury — it is a health necessity. Studies consistently link chronic loneliness in older adults to increased risk of cognitive decline, depression, cardiovascular disease, and premature mortality. For many seniors living alone, days can pass with minimal human contact. MarkoCare's companion care program exists to change that — providing consistent, meaningful presence that makes a measurable difference to overall wellbeing.

## More Than Conversation

Companion care encompasses a wide range of non-medical support activities beyond social engagement. Our caregivers assist with light housekeeping, meal preparation, grocery shopping, errands, and transportation — the practical dimensions of daily life that become harder to manage alone. By handling these tasks, a companion caregiver allows your loved one to redirect energy toward what they enjoy most.

## The Right Match Matters

Not all companionship is equal. MarkoCare takes the matching process seriously — pairing clients with caregivers who share compatible interests, communication styles, and temperaments. A senior who loves gardening and conversation is matched differently from one who prefers quiet company and assistance with reading or correspondence. We invest the time to get this right.

## Supporting Families From a Distance

Many of our companion care clients have adult children who live out of state or are managing demanding work and family schedules of their own. A reliable companion caregiver is not just support for your parent — it is peace of mind for you. Our caregivers observe and report on your loved one's health, home environment, and mood, keeping the family informed without burdening the client.

## When Companion Care Is Combined With Personal Care

Many clients benefit from services that combine companion care and personal care in a single visit. Our RN can design a care plan that integrates both — providing the physical assistance your loved one needs alongside the social connection and oversight that keeps them thriving.

Companion care is available across Howard, Carroll, Anne Arundel, and Frederick Counties, Maryland.
