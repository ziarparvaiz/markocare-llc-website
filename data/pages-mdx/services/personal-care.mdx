Personal care is more than task completion — it is an act of respect for a person's dignity, autonomy, and sense of self. When the ability to bathe independently or manage daily grooming becomes difficult, the emotional weight can be significant. Clients often feel a deep sense of loss when these basic functions require outside help. The right caregiver does not just complete the task — they do so in a way that restores confidence and preserves the client's sense of control over their own life.

## What to Expect From MarkoCare Personal Care

Every MarkoCare personal care client begins with a free in-home assessment led by our Registered Nurse. This assessment goes beyond a checklist — it is a conversation about your loved one's routines, preferences, health history, and goals. The RN then designs an individualized care plan that is tailored to these specifics, not pulled from a template.

Your caregiver is selected based on skills, personality compatibility, and scheduling alignment. We believe consistency matters: whenever possible, you will see the same caregiver at every visit.

## Activities of Daily Living and What They Mean

Activities of daily living (ADLs) — bathing, dressing, grooming, toileting, and mobility — are the foundation of personal independence. Difficulty with these tasks is one of the most common reasons families seek home care. Our caregivers are trained to assist with all ADLs in a way that maximizes what the client can still do independently, rather than taking over entirely.

This approach — called a restorative or dignity-centered model — is clinically supported and reflects our belief that the goal of home care is always to support the person, not simply manage them.

## Working With Your Family

We recognize that family members often serve as informal caregivers themselves — and that bringing in outside help can feel like a complicated emotional decision. Our team is here to support families as much as we support clients. We communicate regularly, answer questions promptly, and adjust care plans as your loved one's needs evolve.

If you have specific instructions, preferences, or concerns, your care coordinator is always accessible to discuss them.

## Maryland's Personal Care Standards

As a Maryland Residential Service Agency operating under COMAR 10.07.05, MarkoCare is held to state standards for personal care delivery. Our RN oversight model exceeds what most private-duty agencies provide — giving your family an additional layer of clinical accountability.

Personal care services are available across Howard, Carroll, Anne Arundel, and Frederick Counties.
