IPOP — the Intensive Outpatient Program at Johns Hopkins — represents a model of post-acute care designed for medically complex patients who require more than standard discharge support. These are patients who cannot safely recover at home without structured, clinically coordinated home care from the first day of discharge.

MarkoCare has built its transitional care program specifically to meet the demands of this kind of complex discharge — with rapid response intake, RN-led care coordination, and direct communication channels with IPOP care teams.

## What Makes IPOP Patients Different

IPOP patients typically present with higher medical complexity, more medications to manage, and a narrower margin for error during the recovery period. A missed follow-up appointment, a medication error, or an unaddressed wound can mean a rapid deterioration and readmission. The caregiving support at home must be proportional to the clinical stakes.

Our RN coordinates with the IPOP discharge team before the patient leaves the hospital, arrives at the home promptly for an intake assessment, and implements a care plan that mirrors the structure and oversight of the IPOP protocol itself.

## Direct Communication With IPOP Teams

MarkoCare maintains structured communication with IPOP care coordinators. Our RN documents client status, flags concerns proactively, and participates in care coordination conversations when needed. The goal is an unbroken chain of clinical oversight from hospital to home.

## For IPOP Discharge Planners

If you are coordinating a complex discharge and need a home care partner that can match the clinical expectations of the IPOP program, we want to work with you. Our intake coordinator is available to receive referrals and begin the placement process with urgency. We offer same-day placement for time-sensitive discharges.

Contact our intake team directly or use our referral partner form to submit a HIPAA-compliant referral. We will respond within hours for urgent cases.

IPOP transitional care support is available across Howard, Carroll, Anne Arundel, and Frederick Counties, Maryland.
