A cancer diagnosis reshapes every aspect of life. Treatment schedules replace normal routines. Fatigue, nausea, and physical limitations change what a person can do independently. Family members step into caregiving roles they may not have anticipated. And through all of it, the person with cancer is trying to hold onto as much of their normal life as possible.

MarkoCare's cancer care support is designed around one purpose: reducing the burden so that patients and families can focus on what matters most.

## Care That Adapts to Treatment

Cancer treatment is not static — it changes week to week, cycle to cycle. Our care plans are designed to adapt. In weeks when treatment side effects are minimal, care can focus on companionship, transportation, and light household support. In harder weeks, more intensive personal care assistance may be needed. Our RN monitors the client's status and adjusts the care plan in real time.

## Immune Suppression and Infection Prevention

Many cancer treatments compromise the immune system, making infection prevention a clinical priority. MarkoCare caregivers assigned to immunocompromised clients receive specific training on hand hygiene protocols, symptom surveillance, and when to escalate concerns to the medical team. We do not treat this as an afterthought — it is a core component of how we care for oncology clients.

## Supporting the Whole Family

A cancer diagnosis affects everyone in the household. Family caregivers often experience their own burnout, grief, and uncertainty. Our respite and companion care options provide relief for family caregivers — not just the patient. We are here for the entire family system.

## Coordination With the Oncology Team

MarkoCare maintains an open line of communication with oncologists, nurses, and care coordinators. If our caregiver observes a change in the client's condition, our RN documents and communicates that observation to the appropriate clinical contact. Early detection of complications during treatment is something we take seriously.

Cancer care support is available across Howard, Carroll, Anne Arundel, and Frederick Counties, Maryland.
