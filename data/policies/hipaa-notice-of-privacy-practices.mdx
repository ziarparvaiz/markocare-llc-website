**THIS NOTICE DESCRIBES HOW MEDICAL INFORMATION ABOUT YOU MAY BE USED AND DISCLOSED AND HOW YOU CAN GET ACCESS TO THIS INFORMATION. PLEASE REVIEW IT CAREFULLY.**

## Our Legal Duty

MarkoCare LLC is required by law to maintain the privacy and security of your Protected Health Information (PHI). We are required to notify you in the event of a breach of your unsecured PHI. We must follow the duties and privacy practices described in this notice and give you a copy of it.

We reserve the right to change our privacy practices and the terms of this notice at any time. If we make a significant change, we will revise this notice and provide it to you upon your next visit or care coordination appointment.

## What Is Protected Health Information?

Protected Health Information (PHI) is any information that:

- Relates to your past, present, or future physical or mental health or condition
- Relates to the provision of health care to you
- Relates to the past, present, or future payment for health care provided to you
- Can be used to identify you (e.g., name, address, date of birth, Social Security number)

## How We May Use and Disclose Your Health Information

### For Treatment

We may use and disclose your PHI to provide, coordinate, or manage your home care services. This includes sharing information with your primary care physician, specialists, or other providers involved in your care, with your authorization.

### For Payment

We may use and disclose PHI to obtain payment for care services provided, including billing insurance companies, Medicare, Medicaid, or other payers on your behalf.

### For Health Care Operations

We may use and disclose PHI for our internal business operations, including quality assessment, employee performance reviews, training, and accreditation activities. These uses are necessary to maintain the quality of care we provide.

### As Required by Law

We will disclose PHI when required to do so by applicable federal, state, or local law, including disclosures to public health agencies, law enforcement, or judicial proceedings.

### For Public Health Activities

We may disclose PHI to public health agencies for disease prevention and control, reporting vital statistics, or as required by law.

### For Abuse or Neglect Reporting

We are required to report suspected instances of abuse, neglect, or domestic violence to appropriate government authorities as mandated by Maryland law.

### For Health Oversight Activities

We may disclose PHI to government agencies responsible for oversight of the health care system, including audits, investigations, and inspections by the Maryland Department of Health.

### In Emergency Situations

We may use or disclose PHI to prevent or lessen a serious and imminent threat to your health or safety, or the health or safety of another person.

### To Business Associates

We may share PHI with vendors and contractors who perform services on our behalf (known as "business associates"), such as billing services or technology providers. All business associates are required to safeguard your PHI under written agreements.

## Uses and Disclosures That Require Your Authorization

We will not use or disclose your PHI for the following purposes without your written authorization:

- Marketing or advertising purposes
- Sale of PHI
- Psychotherapy notes (if applicable)
- Most other uses and disclosures not described in this notice

You may revoke a previously granted authorization at any time by submitting a written request. Revocation will not affect uses or disclosures that occurred prior to the revocation.

## Your Rights Regarding Your Health Information

### Right to Access Your Records

You have the right to inspect and obtain a copy of your PHI maintained by MarkoCare, including electronic records. We may charge a reasonable cost-based fee for copies. We will respond to your request within 30 days.

### Right to Request an Amendment

If you believe PHI in your records is inaccurate or incomplete, you may request an amendment. We may deny the request if we believe the information is accurate and complete, but you may submit a written disagreement for inclusion in your file.

### Right to an Accounting of Disclosures

You have the right to receive a list of certain disclosures of your PHI made by MarkoCare over the past six years, excluding disclosures made for treatment, payment, or health care operations.

### Right to Request Restrictions

You may request restrictions on how we use or disclose your PHI. We are not required to agree to all requested restrictions, but if we do agree, we will honor the restriction.

### Right to Request Confidential Communications

You may request that we communicate with you about health matters in a particular way or at a particular location (e.g., only by email, or only at your home address).

### Right to Receive a Paper Copy of This Notice

You have the right to receive a paper copy of this notice at any time, even if you have received it electronically.

### Right to Notification of Breach

In the event of a breach of your unsecured PHI, we will notify you without unreasonable delay and no later than 60 days after discovery of the breach.
